FNZ Wealth Management Platform

Designing core wealth management modules for a global platform used by major financial institutions — translating highly complex financial workflows into accessible, intuitive interfaces for advisors and operations teams.

My Role
User Experience Designer
Company
FNZ Group, Dublin
Duration
11 Months
Period
Oct 2022 — Aug 2023

Overview

FNZ Group provides a global wealth management platform for major financial institutions — enabling banks, insurers, and wealth managers to deliver investment services to their clients. The platform handles an enormous breadth of complex financial operations: portfolio management, transaction processing, compliance reporting, and client onboarding.

I joined as a UX designer embedded within a cross-functional product team, responsible for designing core wealth management modules that would be used daily by both technical and non-technical financial professionals.

Platform Overview — Core Modules

The Challenge

Wealth management platforms must serve multiple user types simultaneously — from highly technical operations staff who need data density and efficiency, to financial advisors who need clarity and confidence when making decisions that affect client portfolios.

The core challenge was designing workflows around inherently complex financial concepts — rebalancing, tax-wrapper structures, custody chains — in a way that reduced cognitive load without sacrificing the information precision that compliance and operations teams require.

In fintech enterprise design, you can't simplify by removing information — the information is the product. The challenge is presenting it in a way that supports fast, confident decisions rather than creating anxiety.

Process & Collaboration

Working closely with Business Analysts and the development team from day one, I ensured design decisions were grounded in both technical feasibility and regulatory constraint. Complex financial rules (e.g. regulatory reporting requirements, tax optimisation logic) had to be reflected accurately in the UI without overwhelming users with their underlying complexity.

I facilitated design thinking workshops with stakeholders and subject matter experts to frame new features before any design work began — ensuring that development effort was directed at real user and business needs rather than assumed ones.

Design Thinking Workshops & Stakeholder Sessions

For each module, I produced user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity prototypes — presenting at regular design reviews with both technical and business stakeholders to validate decisions before development handoff.

Key Design Decisions

A key pattern across the wealth management modules was a layered information architecture — surfacing summary-level data in primary views, with progressive disclosure into transactional detail as needed. This allowed power users to navigate efficiently while protecting less experienced users from premature complexity.

Core Module Designs — Portfolio & Transaction Views

Status indicators, inline validation, and contextual help were used systematically across high-stakes workflows — ensuring users had confidence at each decision point, particularly during transactions that could not be easily reversed.

Interaction Patterns — Status, Validation & Confirmation States

Outcome & Learnings

The modules I designed were shipped to production and adopted by FNZ's financial institution clients. The design thinking workshops became a repeatable process for the team — used for subsequent feature releases to ensure alignment between business requirements and user needs before development began.

This project deepened my understanding of designing for high-stakes, regulated domains where accuracy matters as much as usability. The closest lesson: in enterprise fintech, trust is a design material — every UI decision is also a decision about how much confidence users will feel when making consequential choices.
