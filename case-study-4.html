Overview

Jobspotting was a job search platform in Berlin. As UX/UI Designer, I owned the product interface through a critical growth phase — designing two major iterations of the web application that significantly improved how job seekers discovered and engaged with opportunities.

Interface Iterations & Job Discovery

I designed two major iterations of the web application interface, each focused on improving job discovery. The challenge was to make the experience more intuitive — helping job seekers find relevant roles without friction and ensuring the product could evolve consistently.

The goal wasn't just to show more jobs — it was to surface the right jobs and make the interface predictable as the product scaled.

First Design System

I created the company's first comprehensive visual style guide and design system. This ensured consistency as the product evolved — defining typography, color, components, and patterns so that new features and future team members could ship with a unified visual language.

User Testing & Validation

I conducted user testing sessions to validate design decisions and understand job seeker behavior. These sessions informed the interface iterations and helped ensure that changes translated into better outcomes for users — not just aesthetic updates.
