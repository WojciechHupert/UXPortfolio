Provenance on Internet

Designed digital ownership and authenticity tracking for creative assets — enabling artists and creators to prove provenance and transfer rights on the blockchain. One of three major products launched from concept to production during Berlin's Web3 emergence.

My Role
UX/UI Designer
Company
ascribe
Duration
3 Years
Period
July 2015 — May 2018

Overview

ascribe was a pioneering blockchain company focused on provenance and authenticity for digital creative work. Artists and creators needed a way to register their work on the blockchain, prove ownership, and transfer rights — concepts that were entirely new to mainstream audiences at the time.

I worked directly with founders to translate the technical vision into user-friendly interfaces. The challenge was explaining blockchain-based provenance to non-technical users through intuitive design, clear information architecture, and strategic simplification of complex concepts.

The Challenge

Everything was being built from first principles. Digital artists, photographers, and creators had no mental model for "owning" digital assets or proving authenticity on a blockchain. The interface had to teach these concepts without overwhelming users with technical jargon.

Provenance design isn't just about tracking — it's about making digital ownership feel tangible and trustworthy to creatives who had never encountered blockchain before.

Process & Impact

I designed and launched this product from concept to production, creating interfaces that let users register works, view provenance history, and transfer ownership in clear, step-by-step flows. I conducted user research and testing sessions to validate that designs resonated with target audiences — iterating based on feedback to improve comprehension and engagement.

I established brand guidelines to ensure visual consistency as the organization scaled. I also collaborated on pitch decks and investor-facing materials that helped communicate the technical innovation clearly and contributed to funding success.

Reception

Users consistently expressed that the interfaces made complex blockchain concepts accessible and engaging. The work impressed stakeholders and contributed to ascribe's early growth and the broader Web3 ecosystem's understanding of provenance for creative assets.
