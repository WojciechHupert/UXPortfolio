Podigee Platform Redesign

Modernising a decade-old podcast hosting platform — rebuilding the design system from scratch, reducing visual noise, and launching AI-powered features that transformed Podigee into a modern industry leader.

My Role
Senior Product Designer
Company
Podigee, Berlin
Duration
15 Months (Ongoing)
Period
Nov 2023 — Present

Overview

Podigee is a leading German podcast hosting and analytics platform. When I joined, the product had accumulated a decade of incremental feature additions without a coherent design language — resulting in a cluttered, inconsistent interface that frustrated users and slowed down development.

My brief was to fully modernise the platform: rationalise the design system, improve user engagement, and introduce new AI-powered features — all while maintaining continuity for existing users.

Platform Before & After — Add screenshots here

The Problem

A decade of product decisions had left the platform visually noisy and technically inconsistent. Components were duplicated across the codebase with no shared source of truth. New feature development was slow because developers had no reliable patterns to reference, and the UI felt dated compared to modern SaaS competitors.

The design system had grown organically for 10 years — it wasn't one system, it was dozens of overlapping micro-decisions made by different people at different times. The task was to find the signal in the noise and build something coherent.

Discovery & Audit

I began with a comprehensive audit of the existing interface — cataloguing all components, identifying duplicates, and mapping user workflows through the core product areas. I cross-referenced with user feedback and support tickets to understand which areas caused the most frustration.

Key findings: the platform had 40+ redundant component variants, inconsistent spacing and typography scales, and several features that received minimal usage but created significant navigational complexity. Underperforming features were flagged for retirement as part of the redesign.

Design Audit — Component Inventory & Usage Analysis

Design System Architecture

I designed and built a new design system comprising 80+ reusable components — from foundational tokens (colour, typography, spacing, elevation) through to complex composed patterns for data tables, navigation, and media players. Every component was built to be accessible, responsive, and documented.

Design System — Component Library Overview

Working closely with the development team, I ensured every design decision was grounded in technical feasibility. The system significantly reduced design-to-development handoff friction and improved UI consistency across the product.

Token Structure & Documentation

AI-Powered SEO Insights

Alongside the design system work, I designed and launched an AI-powered "SEO Insights" feature — enabling podcasters to analyse their episode content quality, identify SEO opportunities, and receive actionable recommendations to improve discoverability.

The challenge was presenting AI-generated data in a way that felt trustworthy, understandable, and actionable for non-technical users. I designed progressive disclosure patterns that surfaced summary scores at a glance, with expandable detail for users who needed to dig deeper.

Dev Velocity

The redesign transformed Podigee from a cluttered, legacy product into a modern industry leader. User satisfaction improved as visual noise was reduced and key workflows were streamlined. Development velocity increased significantly as the team gained reliable, documented patterns to build from. The AI SEO Insights feature was well received as a meaningful product differentiator.

Additionally, I created onboarding video tutorials, animations, and marketing materials to support the platform's go-to-market communications — ensuring the new product experience was communicated effectively to both new and existing users.
